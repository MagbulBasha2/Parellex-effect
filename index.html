Embarking on a trek in the Himalayas is like stepping into a realm of awe-inspiring beauty and challenge. The rugged trails, lush valleys, and snow-capped peaks create an unforgettable backdrop for trekkers

BIKING

Biking Overview: Biking, also known as cycling, involves riding a bicycle or other types of cycles. It’s a versatile activity practiced worldwide for various purposes: Transport: Many people use bicycles for daily commuting and errands. Recreation: Biking provides an enjoyable way to explore scenic routes, parks, and trails.

Standard Bicycles: These have wheels mounted in-line within a metal frame. The rider sits on a saddle, steers using handlebars, and pedals to move the bike. Utility Bicycles: Commonly used for road transport, they have relaxed geometry, mudguards, pannier racks, and lights. They’re practical for daily use.

PARA GLIDING

Paragliding Overview: Paragliding is a sport where participants fly using lightweight, foot-launched gliders called paragliders. These gliders have no rigid structure and rely on the shape of their fabric wings. Pilots sit in a harness beneath the wing, using weight shifts to control their flight. It’s a serene and exhilarating way to experience human flight without an engine

Pilots launch by running off cliffs or hills, harnessing natural lift. The curved wing design allows gliding over long distances and climbing to high altitudes. Paragliding offers a unique perspective, connecting enthusiasts with the elements and the beauty of the skie

SURFING

Surfing Overview: Surfing is the act of riding a wave while standing up on a surfboard. Whether catching waves lying down, kneeling, or standing

Surfers use various types of boards, from shortboards to longboards. By balancing on the moving wave’s forward section, surfers glide toward the shore, enjoying the thrill of riding nature’s energy.
